ANR BIOM

Building Inside/Outside Modeling

The Building Indoor & Outdoor Modelling (BIOM) project aims at automatic, simultaneous indoor and outdoor modelling of buildings from images and dense point clouds. We want to achieve a complete, geometrically accurate, semantically annotated but nonetheless lean 3D CAD representation of buildings and objects they contain in the form of a Building Information Models (BIM) that will help manage buildings in all their life cycle (renovation, simulation, deconstruction). We view indoor and outdoor building modelling as a joint process where both worlds fruitfully cooperate and benefit one another both in terms of semantics and geometry. The hope is that this holistic scene understanding and reconstruction approach will lead to more complete, correct, and geometrically accurate building models.

Le projet Modelisation Intérieur/Extérieur de Bâtiments (BIOM) vise à la modélisation automatique et simultanée de l'intérieur et de l'extérieur de bâtiments à partir de données d'acquisition image et Lidar (nuages de points denses). L'objectif est d'atteindre une représentation 3D complète, précise, sémantisée et simple du bâtiments et des objets fixes qui en font partie (portes, fenêtres, balcons, tuyaux, radiateurs, ...) dans une représentation BIM (Building Information Model) qui permet de gérer tout le cycle de vie du bâtiment. La modélisation intérieur/extérieur est vue comme un processus conjoint où les deux aspect collaborent pour tirer partie de leurs complémentarités sémantiques et géométriques. Cette approche globale permettra une reconstruction plus complète, correcte, précise et cohérente des bâtiments. La première ambition du projet BIOM est d’intégrer des sources de données hétérogènes car la modélisation complète d’un bâtiment requiert des acquisition intérieures et extérieures aériennes et au niveau du sol. Le projet vise aussi à exploiter la complémentarité image/LiDAR et à gérer les occultations dues au mobilier intérieur et urbain. Enfin, une large variété de styles architecturaux, d’arrangements intérieurs et de nombreux types d’objets devront être gérés.

Research Topics

Sujets de recherche

WP1: Registration

WP1 aims at addressing all registration issues faced by the BIOM project, as a major objective of the project is exploiting jointly data from various sensors (image/Lidar) and viewpoints (terrestrial indoor/outdoor, aerial). It is structured into the following subtasks:

  • WP1.a Registration of outdoor heterogeneous data
  • WP1.b Registration of indoor data
  • WP1.c Indoor/Outdoor landmark extraction
  • WP1.d Global indoor/outdoor registration
Coordination: Pascal Monasse

WP2: Outdoor reconstruction

WP2 aims at reconstructing the fine 3D geometry, texture and semantics of a city at large scale from various sensors (image/Lidar) and viewpoints (terrestrial indoor/outdoor, aerial) already registered in WP1.

  • WP2.a Outdoor reconstruction from heterogeneous data
  • WP2.b Outdoor surface texturation from various viewpoints
  • WP2.c Outdoor surface semantization
Coordination: Bruno Vallet, Lastig

WP3: Indoor reconstruction

WP3 aims at preparing all indoor (textured) meshes such that these can enter into landmark recognition (WP1.d) and final joint reconstruction (WP4). A first question will be at what stage we do point meshing. We will investigate whether it is beneficial for our application to stay as long as possible with the original point cloud or whether moving to a mesh at an early stage will bear advantages. To facilitate registration, a coarse semantic classification of objects into relevant categories can be helpful at this early stage to support all later tasks. For example, a rough labeling of points (or mesh triangles) into wall, ceiling, floor, windows, furniture etc. can help precise detection of landmarks and global registration (e.g., walls should be coplanar, ceilings should be on top, floors on bottom). Another system design choice has to be made regarding registration of indoor point clouds. One may first register all indoor data based on raw point clouds [Theiler et al., 2014] and then proceed with texturing, labelling (and meshing) or, alternatively, each room (or even each scan) can be treated separately to leave all entities for global registration including outdoor data in a unified, global framework. Along this line-of-thought, we will investigate if a rough, preliminary geometric consolidation (e.g., with simplifying Manhattan world assumptions) of all (registered) indoor scans can support later landmark extraction and global registration to outdoor data.

  • WP3.a Indoor reconstruction from low cost data
  • WP3.b Indoor reconstruction from fixed Lidar stations
  • WP3.c Indoor surface texturation
  • WP3.d Indoor surface semantization
Coordination: Florent Lafarge, Titane

WP4: Structured combined reconstruction

WP4 aims at the final integrated BIM modelling from both inside and outside semantized reconstructions. The output of WP4 is an automatically generated, semantically annotated, seamless indoor-outdoor, geometrically precise 3D building model. It is structured into the following subtasks:

  • WP4.a Indoor/Outdoor object recognition and reconstruction
  • WP4.b Fine indoor/outdoor reconstruction
  • WP4.c Outdoor/indoor BIM simplification
Coordination: Pierre Grussenmeyer, TRIO

WP5: Coordination and applications

As applications will involve a strong implication of all partners, project global coordination and application specific coordination are conducted within the same work package. WP5 aims to ensure global coordination of all research effort during the life of the BIOM project, and in particular give guidance to WP1-4 work, in order to validate the results and experiment their integration with real-life issues and domain applications, frequently encountered by using BIM/GIS models in building or urban renovation projects.

  • WP5.a Project coordination
  • WP5.b Adequacy to uses and data acquisition
  • WP5.c Ground truth
  • WP5.d Test use cases
  • WP5.e Industrial Proof of Concept
Coordination: Sven Oesau

Consortium

LASTIG

IGN France

The Lastig lab is a mixed research unit working on geographical information sciences. IGN is the French reference on geographical information and was one of the first NMA (National Mapping Agency) to set up a 3D city model production in 2008, based on its research. Since then, the Lastig has had a strong activity on urban scene analysis and reconstruction, especially from mobile mapping data (both image and Lidar).

Participants: Bruno Vallet, Clément Mallet, Loïc Landrieu, Laurent Caraffa

Saint Mandé, France

IMAGINE

ENPC, LIGM lab

The IMAGINE team at LIGM has been working for many years on 3D reconstruction from images and Lidar, including calibration, with an emphasis on building reconstruction and semantization, using a variety of approaches (shape grammars, MRFs, deep learning). Imagine is a research team from the LIGM lab, depending on Ecole Nationale des Ponts et Chaussées ParisTech

Participants: Renaud Marlet, Pascal Monasse

Champs sur Marne, France

TRIO

INSA Strasbourg, iCube lab

The TRIO team from ICube lab at INSA-Strasbourg will bring its strong experience and expertise in the reconstruction of indoor scenes in the context of as-built BIM creation

Participants: Pierre Grussenmeyer, Tania Landes

Strasbourg, France

TITANE

INRIA Sophia Antipolis

The Titane team of INRIA is one of the leading group in robust geometry processing in the world, with expertise in surface reconstruction and approximation as well as indoor reconstruction and urban modeling.

Participants: Florent Lafarge, Pierre Alliez

Sophia Antipolis, France

MIC

CSTB

The CSTB is the French reference on BIM and has initiated a collaboration with IGN on BIM/GIS (Geographical Information System) convergence for which the BIOM project could be the perfect experimental context. Its MIC team has a strong expertise in building modeling and its link with physical simulations.

Participants: Sven Oesau, Julien Soula, Guillaume Picinbono, Arnaud Mistre

Sophia Antipolis, France

Eurometropole Strasbourg

The project is supported by the European Metropole of Strasbourg who provides high resolution oblique aerial imagery and defined the test sites on its territory.

Sophia Antipolis, France

The complementarity of this consortium first relies on the presence of the public actors responsible for city (IGN) and building (CSTB) modeling. From a scientific perspective, the members of the consortium cover all the methodologies required to achieve the ambitious goals: computer vision (LIGM/Imagine), photo- and laser-grammetry (IGN/MATIS, INSA/TRIO), computer graphics and geometry processing (INRIA/Titane). The consortium also covers from this methodological expertise to practical applications with the experience of INSA/TRIO on indoor structures extraction and CSTB/MIC on BIM and its usages.
